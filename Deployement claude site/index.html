About the Legend

When you play for India, you have 1.3 billion people behind you.

Mahendra Singh Dhoni — born 7 July 1981 in Ranchi, Jharkhand — rose from a ticket collector at Kharagpur railway station to become the most decorated captain in Indian cricket history.

Known for his ice-cold nerves, helicopter shot, and lightning-fast stumpings, Dhoni redefined what it means to lead under pressure. The 2011 World Cup final six off Nuwan Kulasekara will live forever in Indian memory.

He retired from international cricket in August 2020, but his legacy endures through a generation of cricketers he inspired and millions of fans whose hearts he captured forever.

Test Cricket
